# Moderated Usability Test Script — BoazPlan

**Study:** BoazPlan — core flows (New Request, Draft/Offline, Pastor Approval, Volunteer Assignment)
**Method:** Remote moderated usability test (recorded)
**Session length:** 45–60 minutes
**Facilitator:** [Name]
**Participant roles:** Member / Pastor / Volunteer (use role-specific tasks)

---

## Before the session (facilitator checklist)
- Confirm participant consent and recording permission.
- Share prototype link and ensure mobile device used (mobile-first PWA flows).
- Open session platform (Zoom/Teams) and enable screen-recording.
- Keep a notes template ready for task metrics and observations.

---

## Welcome & Consent (3 min)
Script: "Thanks — I’m [name]. We’re testing an early prototype of a church help & prayer app. There are no right or wrong answers; we’re testing the design, not you. This session will be recorded for internal analysis. Do you consent to the recording and to us taking notes?"
If yes, continue. If no, stop.

Quick demographic questions (1–2 min):
- What is your role in your church? (member, pastor, volunteer, leader)
- How comfortable are you with smartphone apps? (1–5)

---

## Pre-test questions (2 min)
- How do you currently coordinate help or prayer requests in your church?
- What concerns do you have about privacy or approval when posting sensitive requests?

---

## Tasks (record time & success)
Note to facilitator: Read task scenario aloud. Ask participant to think aloud. Do not help unless they ask for clarification.

Task A — Primary path: Create & submit a Request (Mobile) (10 min)
- Scenario: "Imagine a neighbor needs a ride to a medical appointment. Please post a request that explains the need, select visibility to 'Group', and submit."
- Success = request created, visibility set correctly, confirmation seen.
- Measures: time-on-task, success/failure, errors, hesitation points.
- Post-task probe: "What, if anything, was confusing? How would you improve the form?"

Task B — Edge case: Draft & offline recovery (7 min)
- Scenario: "Start a request and simulate a spotty connection (we'll pause and then resume). Save as draft, close, reopen the app and resume submission."
- Success = draft saved, resumed, submitted after reconnect.
- Measures: detection of offline state, clarity of save/restore actions, errors.
- Post-task probe: "Did you feel confident the draft was saved? What wording would make it clearer?"

Task C — Pastor Approval Flow & Privacy (8 min) — (for Pastor participants)
- Scenario: "As Pastor, find pending requests, open one flagged for review, set visibility to 'Group only' and approve/decline."
- Success = request approved/declined and audit entry visible.
- Measures: discoverability of approval queue, clarity of privacy toggles, time to decision.
- Post-task probe: "Would you trust this interface to make privacy decisions? What would you change?"

Task D — Volunteer Assignment & Update (8 min) — (for Volunteer participants)
- Scenario: "You receive a volunteer assignment. Accept it, update it to 'in-progress', then mark it 'completed'."
- Success = accept/decline and status updates recorded.
- Measures: clarity of notification, ease of status updates, expected fields.
- Post-task probe: "What information did you need but couldn't find?"

---

## Final interview / subjective ratings (7–10 min)
- SUS-style quick score: How easy was the product to use? (1–5)
- Likelihood to use / recommend (1–5)
- Privacy/trust question: Do you trust the app to protect sensitive requests? (Y/N + why)
- Open feedback: What was the biggest friction? What would make you use this weekly?

---

## Data & metrics to capture
- Quantitative: task completion time, success rate, error count, SUS-like rating, median time vs 2-minute target.  
- Qualitative: main pain points, privacy concerns, suggested copy changes, observed hesitations.
